The Presentation Project

Use actor's methods to develop your presentation skills.

Do you remember your very first presentation?

Maybe you were nervous and wished to speak with more self-confidence, or to answer questions with more certainty.

And today, after years of work experience, you still get agitated, when having to speak and wish for more security in your presentations?

No matter how your beginnings looked like, we will start at the base and will make sure that you will not only be successful but also satisfied with any presentation you will give.

Your body will always communicate

You just have to make it say the same thing you do

If your body language is giving a completely different story, it doesn’t matter how genius your ideas are, because they won’t arrive in the minds of the people listening to you.

With the Presentation Project I want to help you to fully develop your impact during your presentations, that one super important pitch, but also in your everyday life.

Team

Here we playfully train the soft skills that are indispensable for presenting, but also for communicating in the team.

  • Interaction as a team
  • Quick reaction
  • Body and expression
  • Stage presence

Due to the many playful elements from improvisational theater, this offer is also perfect as a team building event. Alternatively, we can place the emphasis on one of the other sub-areas.

Tête-à-tête

The one-on-one training to get your presentation TEDTalk-ready.

We work on your body language, facial expressions, presence, voice, and language and go through your presentation together step by step.

But we also go into storytelling: What story do you want to tell with your presentation?

If you haven't found the story within your topic yet, we'll figure it out together!

Through exercises from improvisational theater, acting, and speech training, you will learn to…

  • Use your body language in a targeted way to radiate self-confidence and professionalism, and convince everyone from your ideas.
  • Make your presentation exciting and with the help of storytelling techniques captivate everyone around you.
  • Control your voice and breathing to focus attention on what's really important.
  • Respond spontaneously to even the trickiest follow-up questions while remaining professional without becoming nervous.
  • Communicate your ideas clearly, so that everyone in the room understands, why they urgently need your solution, your product, your service.
